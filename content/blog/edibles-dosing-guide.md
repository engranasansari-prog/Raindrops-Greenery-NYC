## What the mg numbers on the package actually mean

Flower Strains are labeled in THC% — the percentage of THC by dry weight. Edibles speak a different language entirely: **milligrams of THC**. Three numbers matter on any edible label:

- **Per-piece dose** — the mg of THC in one gummy, chocolate square, or cookie. Common per-piece doses are 2.5mg, 5mg, and 10mg, with some products running 25mg or more per piece.
- **Total package dose** — per-piece dose multiplied by the piece count. A "100mg pack" usually means ten pieces at 10mg each, not one 100mg gummy.
- **The score line** — many gummies are molded to snap cleanly in half. Half of a 5mg gummy is a 2.5mg dose, which is exactly how a lot of careful first-timers begin.

The most useful habit you can build is reading **per-piece first**. The package total tells you what's in the bag; the per-piece number tells you what you're about to feel. Some products also blend CBD alongside THC — when they do, the ratio (like 1:1) is printed on the label next to the mg numbers.

## How many mg of edibles for beginners?

The starting point you'll see across nearly every educational resource is the same: **2.5 to 5mg of THC**. That's not a prescription — it's the widely used low end, and the logic behind it is simple. If a dose turns out to be too gentle, the fix is cheap: adjust next time. If a dose turns out to be too strong, there's no undo button — you're on that ride for hours. Beginners are better off erring small.

Here's the general map of how doses are commonly described, in sensory terms:

- **1–2.5mg** — a feather-light touch. Many people feel very little their first time at this level, and that's fine — it's a calibration run.
- **2.5–5mg** — the classic first dose. A gentle shift: conversational, easygoing, relaxing-evening territory for most newcomers.
- **5–10mg** — clearly noticeable. This is where many regular consumers settle once they know their response.
- **10–20mg** — strong. Built for experienced consumers with an established tolerance.
- **20mg and up** — experienced territory only. Not a first-night number, full stop.

One more thing worth saying plainly: **smoking experience doesn't transfer**. If you've enjoyed flower or pre-rolls for years but never eaten an edible, start at 5mg or less anyway. Your body processes an eaten dose through a completely different route, and a smoking tolerance doesn't map onto it one-to-one.

## Why edibles feel different from smoking

When you smoke, THC reaches your bloodstream through your lungs and you feel it within minutes. When you eat an edible, the dose travels through your stomach and liver first, where THC is converted into a different compound — 11-hydroxy-THC — that tends to feel heavier per milligram and lasts considerably longer.

That digestive route is why the timeline looks nothing like smoking:

- **Onset** — anywhere from 30 minutes to 2 hours. What and when you last ate shifts this window noticeably.
- **Peak** — often 2 to 4 hours after eating.
- **Duration** — commonly 4 to 8 hours from start to finish.

Slow onset is the single biggest thing beginners misread. The edible isn't weak. It isn't broken. It's in transit.

## The one rule every edibles dosing guide repeats: don't re-dose early

The classic beginner mistake happens at minute 45. Nothing yet, so it "must not be working," so a second gummy goes down — and then both doses arrive together an hour later. That's how a planned 5mg evening becomes a 15mg evening nobody asked for.

The fix is a hard rule, not a vibe: **wait at least two hours from your first dose before even considering more**. And honestly, on a first night, the better play is to not re-dose at all — call the evening a data point, write down what you took and how it went, and adjust the number next time.

## What to do while you wait

Set yourself up so the wait is easy and the landing is soft:

- **Set a timer when you eat the dose.** Memory gets unreliable on this exact question.
- **Eat a normal meal or snack first.** A fed stomach makes for a slower, smoother arrival than an empty one.
- **Queue something long.** A movie, an album, a familiar series — something that doesn't need you to make decisions.
- **Stay home, or somewhere you're fully comfortable.** First doses and unfamiliar places are a bad pairing.
- **Keep water within reach.** Dry mouth is real.
- **Skip the wine.** Alcohol tends to turn the volume up on an edible, and a first run is much easier to read without it.
- **Do not drive.** Not during the wait, not after it kicks in. Plan the whole evening around staying put.

## Common questions

**Should my first edible be on a full or empty stomach?**
With food in your system, the arrival tends to be slower and smoother; on an empty stomach it tends to come on faster and sharper. Most beginners have an easier time reading their dose after a normal meal.

**What if I took too much?**
You can't un-eat it, but you can make the ride comfortable: water, a snack, a familiar show, and somewhere cozy to settle in. The intensity peaks and then fades on its own over a few hours. Don't drive anywhere, and don't add more of anything — time is what moves it along.

**How should I store edibles?**
In their original child-resistant packaging, in a cool, dark spot, firmly out of reach of children and pets. Gummies look like candy to anyone who doesn't know better — never leave them sitting out.
