When you check out at Raindrops, you don't see a New York State cannabis excise tax line. You don't see the 9% retail surcharge. You don't see the 13% adult-use combined rate the rest of the state pays. The price on the card is the price at the door.

That isn't a trick. It isn't a loophole. It's how sovereign cannabis works in New York.

## The Shinnecock Indian Nation Cannabis Regulatory Division

Raindrops Greenery operates under a cannabis license issued by the **Shinnecock Indian Nation Cannabis Regulatory Division** — the regulatory body of a federally recognized sovereign tribe with its own land base on the East End of Long Island.

Sovereign tribal nations in the United States have the right to regulate commerce — including cannabis — on their land and through licensees they choose to authorize. That regulatory authority is independent of the New York State Office of Cannabis Management (OCM), which licenses non-sovereign retailers across the state.

The Shinnecock Nation gained federal recognition in 2010 after a multi-decade legal effort. Federal recognition means the U.S. government treats the tribe as a sovereign nation for purposes of self-governance, regulation, and commerce. The Shinnecock launched their own cannabis regulatory framework, and the dispensary serving the tribal land base opened in 2023.

Licensees of the Shinnecock cannabis program — Raindrops Greenery among them — operate under that sovereign authority, with the tribe's testing, tracking, and compliance requirements rather than the state's. Different framework, different rule book, same federally legal cannabis.

## What this means for the price you pay

OCM-licensed New York dispensaries are required to charge:

- A **9% retail excise tax** on cannabis products
- The standard **NY State + city sales tax** stack (around 8.875% in NYC)

That's roughly 13% added on top of the menu price by the time you hit checkout. On a $100 order at an OCM dispensary, you're paying about $113 once the math is done.

Sales conducted under the Shinnecock cannabis program are **not subject to those state-level cannabis taxes**. So when a [Raindrops drop arrives at your door](/delivery):

- The product price is the menu price
- Delivery is free on orders over $25 — no fee, no minimum game
- No state cannabis excise, no state sales tax

A $100 order at Raindrops is $100. That's it. The math you do in your head before tapping checkout is the math the driver hands over.

## Is this legal?

Yes. Tribal sovereignty over commerce is recognized in U.S. federal law and has been since *Worcester v. Georgia* in 1832 — the Supreme Court ruling that affirmed tribal nations as distinct political communities with the right to self-govern on their land. The Indian Commerce Clause of the U.S. Constitution (Article I, Section 8) gives Congress sole authority to regulate trade with tribes, which by extension means state-level regulators don't override tribal authority on tribal matters.

For full clarity on what that means in practice:

- Customers must still be **21 or older** with a valid government ID — verified at the door, no exceptions
- Products are still subject to **Shinnecock cannabis program testing and tracking** rules — every product is regulated, just by a different agency
- We can only deliver inside our **licensed service area** — Manhattan plus parts of Brooklyn (Williamsburg, Greenpoint) and Queens (Long Island City)

The phrase "tax-free cannabis" gets thrown around loosely. We use it for one reason: the New York State cannabis excise tax and state sales tax are not applied to your order. That's the specific, verifiable, documented benefit.

## Common questions

**Do you pay any tribal taxes or fees?**
Yes. Operating under a Shinnecock cannabis license involves regulatory fees that go to the tribe — that's how the program is funded. Those fees are not passed through as a checkout line item; they're built into our cost structure. The menu price is the price.

**Can the state ever come after this?**
The state has not asserted authority over tribal cannabis commerce conducted under federally recognized sovereign frameworks. Federal law is clear on the point, and the Shinnecock cannabis program has operated since 2023 without state interference.

**Why don't all delivery services do this?**
Most NYC cannabis delivery services operate under OCM licenses or no license at all (the unregulated market). Operating under a Shinnecock cannabis license requires a direct relationship with the tribe and compliance with the tribe's program — it's not a flag you can just wave. We did the work to set it up.

**Will pricing ever change?**
Menu prices change as our cost basis changes, like any retail business. The **tax-free** status is structural — it's tied to operating under the Shinnecock license, which is how we're set up to do business permanently.
