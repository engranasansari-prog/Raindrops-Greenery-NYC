When you check out at Raindrops, you don't see a New York State cannabis excise tax line. You don't see the 9% retail surcharge. You don't see the 13% adult-use combined rate the rest of the state pays. The price on the card is the price at the door.

That isn't a trick. It isn't a loophole. It's how sovereign cannabis works in New York.

## The Shinnecock Indian Nation Cannabis Regulatory Division

Raindrops Greenery operates under a cannabis license issued by the **Shinnecock Indian Nation Cannabis Regulatory Division** — the regulatory body of a federally recognized sovereign tribe with its own land base on the East End of Long Island.

Sovereign tribal nations in the United States have the right to regulate commerce — including cannabis — on their land and through licensees they choose to authorize. That regulatory authority is independent of the New York State Office of Cannabis Management (OCM), which licenses non-sovereign retailers.

## What this means for the price you pay

OCM-licensed New York dispensaries are required to charge:

- A **9% retail excise tax** on cannabis products
- The standard **NY State + city sales tax** stack (around 8.875% in NYC)

That's roughly 13% added on top of the menu price by the time you hit checkout.

Sales conducted under the Shinnecock cannabis program are **not subject to those state-level cannabis taxes**. So when a Raindrops drop arrives at your door:

- The product price is the menu price
- Delivery is free (no fee, no minimum)
- No state cannabis excise, no state sales tax

## Is this legal?

Yes. Tribal sovereignty over commerce is recognized in U.S. federal law and has been since the founding of the Constitution. The Shinnecock Indian Nation has operated a cannabis regulatory framework since their dispensary launched in 2023, and the regulatory division publishes licensed operators and product testing requirements.

For full clarity:

- Customers must still be **21 or older** with a valid government ID — verified at the door
- Products are still subject to **Shinnecock cannabis program testing and tracking** rules
- We can only deliver inside our licensed service area (Manhattan + LIC + Williamsburg + Greenpoint)

## What you actually save

For a typical $80 order at an OCM dispensary, you'd pay roughly $90 after taxes. Order the same products at Raindrops, you pay $80. That ~$10–12 difference per order — plus the free delivery — is the practical benefit of buying through a Shinnecock-licensed operator.

We didn't build this site to dodge taxes. We built it because cannabis customers in New York have been overpaying for legal access, and there's a better path that's both legal and respectful to the tribal authority that makes it possible.
