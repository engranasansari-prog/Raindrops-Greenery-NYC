Scroll any dispensary menu — ours included — and three words do most of the sorting: **indica**, **sativa**, **hybrid**. The indica vs sativa vs hybrid question is the first one almost every new customer asks us, and it deserves a straight answer. The labels are genuinely useful, as long as you know what they actually promise — and what they don't. Here's the plain-English version, plus how the badges work on the Raindrops Greenery menu.

## Indica vs sativa vs hybrid: where the labels come from

The names started as botany. In the 1700s, naturalists classified two cannabis plant types: *sativa*, the tall, lanky plants from equatorial regions, and *indica*, the short, bushy plants from mountain climates. The names described how the plants grew — not how they felt.

Two centuries of crossbreeding later, almost every strain on the market is genetically a mix of both. Pure landrace indicas and sativas are collector's items, not menu staples. So why do the labels survive? Because somewhere along the way, they stopped describing plant shape and started describing **expected experience**. Today, when a grower labels a strain Indica, they're telling you which direction the experience leans — and that shorthand, used honestly, is still the fastest way to navigate a menu.

## What each label actually tells you

Think of the three labels as directions on a dial, not three separate boxes.

- **Indica** — shorthand for a body-forward, settled experience. The classic indica evening is couch-cozy: a blanket gains gravity, the movie gets better, the to-do list politely excuses itself. Most people reach for indicas at night.
- **Sativa** — shorthand for a head-forward, lively experience. Sativas are the social-energy pick: conversation flows easier, playlists sound more interesting, a walk through the neighborhood feels like an event. Most people reach for sativas earlier in the day or before plans.
- **Hybrid** — the balanced middle, and the biggest category by far. A good hybrid gives you a bit of body ease and a bit of mental lift without committing hard to either. It's the most forgiving place to start and the category most modern strains genuinely belong to.

Between the poles sit the leaners: **Indica-Hybrid** (settled, but not glued to the couch) and **Sativa-Hybrid** (lively, but not racing). On our menu those middle categories do a lot of quiet work — they're often the right answer when a pure indica or sativa feels like too strong a commitment.

## The honest limits of the labels

Here's the part many menus skip: strain type is a tendency, not a guarantee. Three things the label can't tell you on its own.

- **Genetics are blended.** Because nearly everything is a hybrid at the DNA level, two strains wearing the same Indica badge can feel noticeably different. The label describes the lean, not a precise destination.
- **Terpenes do heavy lifting.** The aromatic compounds that give each strain its character — citrus, pine, gas, baked dough — shape the experience as much as the indica/sativa lineage does. A bright, citrus-forward indica can feel livelier than a heavy, earthy hybrid. If a jar smells appealing to you, that's real data.
- **You are a variable.** Tolerance, setting, time of day, and what you've eaten all bend the experience. The same sativa that powers a Saturday afternoon with friends can feel like too much chatter on a quiet Tuesday night.

None of this makes the labels useless. It makes them a starting point — the first filter, not the final word. Recommendations are educational, not medical advice. Cannabis affects each person differently. Start low, go slow — especially with edibles.

## Which strain type should I choose?

If you're staring at the menu wondering which strain type you should choose, work backward from the evening you want.

- **A chill night in** — movie, takeout, no plans until morning. Reach for an **Indica** or **Indica-Hybrid**. This is the lowest-stakes way to experience what "body-forward" means.
- **Social plans** — a rooftop, a dinner, a long catch-up call. A **Sativa** or **Sativa-Hybrid** keeps the energy conversational instead of cozy.
- **A creative session** — cooking something ambitious, sketching, rearranging the apartment at 8pm. **Sativa-Hybrids** are the classic creative-spark pick; they lift without scattering.
- **First time, or first time in a while** — start with a **Hybrid** at a moderate THC% (15–22%). It's the most balanced read on what you like, and you can lean indica or sativa on the next order once you know.
- **Genuinely unsure** — let the menu come to you. It takes about 30 seconds and it's the exact conversation our team would have with you in person.

One honest tip: when two strains both fit your scenario, pick the one whose aroma description appeals to you more. Your nose is a better strain-matcher than most people give it credit for.

## Common questions

**Is an indica always relaxing?**
It's a strong tendency, not a promise. Genetics, terpenes, dose, and your own state all matter. If you want the most reliably mellow evening, pick an Indica or Indica-Hybrid with a moderate THC% and keep the first session small.

**What does Indica-Hybrid actually mean?**
A strain whose lineage and character lean indica without going full couch-mode. It's our most-recommended badge for people who want a relaxed evening but still plan to finish the movie.

**Do sativa strains feel too intense for a quiet night?**
They can feel more lively than the moment calls for, which isn't the same as intense. If your evening is a wind-down, an indica-leaning pick simply matches the mood better.

**Does THC% matter more than strain type?**
They answer different questions. Strain type sets the direction (cozy vs lively), THC% sets the volume. A 19% indica and a 28% indica point the same way — one just speaks much louder.

**Can I mix types in one order?**
Absolutely, and regulars often do — an indica for weeknights, a sativa-hybrid for the weekend. Minimum for free delivery is just $25.
